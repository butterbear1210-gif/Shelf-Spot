Books On The Move

Community Reading Space

After exploring libraries and bookstores, I wanted to experience something a little different. That's what led me to Books on the Move at The LINC KL, a community book sharing space where books are donated, exchanged, and passed on to new readers. Unlike a traditional bookstore or library, this place encourages people to give pre-loved books a second life. I really liked the idea because it reminds us that every book still has a story to share, no matter how many hands it has passed through.

Here It Is!

This was the first thing I saw after reaching Books on the Move. Compared to the libraries and bookstores I had visited before, this place felt much simpler. Instead of rows of brand-new books, the shelves were filled with pre-loved books waiting to be discovered by their next reader.

Close look

As I explored the space a little more, I learned that Books on the Move is more than just a place to read. I also noticed that this project is supported through collaborations with several organisations, including RapidKL, Think City, Yayasan Hasanah, MIA, and Merchantrade Asia, showing how different communities have come together to promote reading and make books more accessible to everyone.

My visit may have come to an end, but the idea behind Books on the Move stayed with me. It's a simple reminder that sharing books is also a way of sharing knowledge, experiences, and stories with others. Hopefully, more people will continue supporting initiatives like this so that every book can find its next reader.
