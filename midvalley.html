BookXcess-The Gardens Rooftop

Fresh air, fresh restart

After visiting a few BookXcess outlets, I realised that the two branches not feel exactly the same. Each one has its own design, atmosphere, and little details that make the experience memorable. So this time, I decided to explore BookXcess: The Rooftop to see what made this particular outlet stand out from the rest.

A long walk

Getting here was surprisingly easy. I took the KTM and got off at Abdullah Hukum Station before walking directly into The Gardens Mall through the connected walkway. From there, I simply took the lift to Level 6, followed the short rooftop walkway, and within a few minutes... I had finally arrived at BookXcess: The Rooftop!

One step closer

After a short walk along the rooftop pathway, I was welcomed by such a beautiful view. Since I arrived in the morning, the weather was still cool, making the walk even more enjoyable. It felt like a peaceful little escape right in the middle of the city.

The rooftop itself was worth exploring. Besides the beautiful city view, I was surprised by how peaceful the surroundings felt. The blooming flowers immediately caught my eye, while the fountain, seating area, and swimming pool below made the whole place feel calm and refreshing. I ended up sitting there for a while just to enjoy the scenery.

Books Around Every Corner

Rather than using a typical bookstore layout, BookXcess made full use of the stepped floor design. The bookshelves were arranged to follow each level, creating a space that felt open, organised, and enjoyable to explore. I found myself slowing down naturally because every few steps brought a new section of books into view.

Before I Leave...

Before ending my visit, I spent a little more time looking around this cosy reading area. The long tables and comfortable seating make it a nice place to sit, read, or simply take a short break. If you get hungry or thirsty, there's also a small café inside the bookstore where you can grab a drink or a light meal before continuing your visit. I thought it was a nice touch, especially for those planning to spend a few hours here.
